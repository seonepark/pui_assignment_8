Changing the Paradigm for Recycling

Design Challenge :

How can we change the paradigm for recycling?

Emphasis :

Cultivating empathy towards users, user research and synthesis, idating and refining concepts, prototyping ideas, user testing, and data analysis

Project Length :

10 weeks

Team Members :

Edward Li, Raghav Mehta, Francisco Mejia, Ryan Nicanor, Annie Park, Tyler Sorensen

Role :

User Research Lead

The Situation

There have been numerous attempts to teach and enforce recycling in America. We have reached a point in history where most people are taught to recycle, the benefits of recycling, and the consequences of not recycling; however, we are not recycling.

Design Solution

Our design solution is an art piece that draws inspiration from things like the "Before I Die Wall" and the "Gum Wall". It will consist of 4 receptacles shaped like the alphabetic letters U,C,S,D. Students will place their recyclable materials inside the cabinets.

This infographic serves as a more explicit method for informing users about proper recycling protocol. Ideally, this would be similar to tourists reading the descriptions of museum exhibits.

Understand - Exploration

Become an expert in the Recycle Problem

Goal :

Secondary research on recycling to learn... How many people recycle in California? What are the rules to recycling? Why do people not recycle? What have been done to combat this problem? Is there a solution to our current recycling problem?

What we learned :

- Only 58% of northeast-American residents and 56% of western-American residents actually recycle in the United States.
- The top five reasons people don't recycle are:
1) Accessibility of recycling plant
2) Toomuch time conumption
3) Forgetfulness
4) Lack of knowledge of what is and isn't recyclable and
5) Cost. - Macro and micro approach to solving this problem.
1991 e-Waste fee in California, and redesign of trash cans.

Understand - Empathize

Needfinding with San Diegans

Goal :

To learn how students, faculty, staff, and average adults view recycling, and if they recycle or not.

What we learned :

Spent 3 hours observing around the UCSD campus and interviewed around 50 people collectively as a team. We learned that most of our interviewees have only a vague idea of what is recyclable and what it not. Many wrer unsure about what to do with grey area items, such as containers with food scraps. Even the individuals who wrer more informed about recycling admiited that they don't think deep into what is recyclable when they have other tasks.

Implement - Prototype

Make mistakes early, and learn from them!

Goal :

Parallel prototypes to test different dimensions of our design solution

Outcome :

Our first prototype idea was to redesign the physical trashcans at UCSD to better promote salience. We wanted to see if changing the physical features of the trashcan will 1) change user behavior 2) educate usres on what is recyclable. Our second prototype was to combat the problem of salience and ignorance of rules by making a Recycle Wall that would function as a trashcan for students going to class, but also become a wall to educate people on what is considered recyclable.

Test - User Testing

Recognize the Flaws from Testing with Users

Goal :

Understand the flaws of our prototypes by testing with students.

What worked :

- Prototype 1 : Salience - transparent material was helpful because of the constant visual feedback.
- Prototype 2 : Easy to interact with and educates through constant visual feedback.

What we learned :

Prototype 1 : testers questioned feasibility resulting from the cost of constantly replacing material.
Prototype 2 : What happens if it is windy? who cleans up the mess? No stable structure to keep the trash together.
