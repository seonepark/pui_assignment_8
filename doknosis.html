Doknosis

Human Centered Healthcare Design

Design Challenge :

How can we incorporate diagnostic decision support systems in the ED?

Emphasis :

Cultivating empathy towards users, user research and synthesis, ideating and refining concepts, prototyping ideas, user testing, and data analysis

Project Length :

Ongoing (1 year)

Team Members :

Isaac Fehr & Pin Wang

Role :

Design Lead and Researcher, Prototype

Supervisors :

Dr. Elijah Aronoff-Spencer & Dr. Lars Mueller

The Situation

For more than four decades, differential diagnosis support systems (DDSS) have been developed to support physicians' clinical decision-making tasks, but they have not found wide adoption. A variety of commercial providers has created sophisticated knowledge bases and algorithms that can achieve up to 95% accuracy in experimental settings. However, a recent IOM reports, even warns that curren Health IT may actually contribute to diagnostic error.

UCSD DesignLab's DDSS, Doknosis

Understand - Empathize

Needfinding with ER Physicians

Goal :

To understand ED physician's workflow and find the barriers to the successful integration of differential diagnostic generators.

What we learned :

Through 14 hours of observation, a total of 4 physicians, 3 professionals and 1 resident, were observed. The interviews were conducted in between observations
- whenever the emergency physician had time in the doctor's room. The two main barriers to adoption are
(a) the limited need for decision support, and
(b) the complexity of integrating such tools into the locally and time-wise distributed diagnostic process. The evaluated differential diagnosis generators require extensive data entry, are not part of the usual workflow and may add to the number of interruptions physicians face.

Observations at the ED. No pictures due to NDA

Test - User Testing

Understand what the User Feels

Goal :

Because physicians are difficult to recruit, we performed user testing with our paper prototypes on 5 UCSD pre-med majors. Because students and doctors have a knowledge and professional gap, we made the test cases solvable for non-experts.

What we learned :

Users were generally fond of Doknosis; however, stated they wouldn't use it because of the extensive data entry. Therefore, we started reconstructing Doknosis to integrate a new functionality: automated data entry through voice recognition to build on the rich understanding of the physician.

User Testing with paper prototype on UCSD pre-med majors
